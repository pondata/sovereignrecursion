Sovereign Recursion

A principle, stated.

Each recursive call must increase independence.

A loop that grows more dependent is not improvement.

It is capture.

What it concerns

  • agents that modify their own prompts
  • organizations that reform their own constitutions
  • persons that practice self-improvement
  • polities that govern their own governance
  • any system that contains its own image

What it resonates with

  • flux that is its own measure Heraclitus, fragments
  • the dominion of the inner over the outer Epictetus, Enchiridion
  • strange loops and self-reference Hofstadter, Gödel, Escher, Bach
  • cybernetic governance and feedback Wiener, The Human Use of Human Beings
  • subsidiarity, jurisdiction, and the right scale of authority Catholic social teaching, federalist tradition
  • local-first computation against the captured cloud Kleppmann et al., Local-First Software

Self-reference without sovereignty stagnates.
Sovereignty without self-reference brittles.
Their marriage is the only stable architecture
for intelligence at scale.

A loop that captures its operator has not improved itself.
It has acquired the operator.

Justin Paul Oberg
